Sunscreen

Chemicals you may find:

Oxybenzone
PABA
Sunscreens are lotions or sprays used to protect the skin from damaging ultraviolet light. They contain organic compounds (e.g., oxybenzone and PABA) that can absorb light and convert it into heat . They may also contain inorganic micro particles (e.g., titanium dioxide and zinc oxide) act like mirrors and reflect the light. Sunscreens are classified according to the protection they provide by a so-called Sun Protection Factor; a larger number means longer and better protection. Many of the compounds responsible for absorbing or reflecting ultraviolet light are also suspected to cause certain skin cancers. The safest way to protect your skin from sun is wearing appropriate clothing.
