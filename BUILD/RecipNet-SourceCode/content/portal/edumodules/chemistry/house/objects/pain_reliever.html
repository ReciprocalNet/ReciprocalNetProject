Pain relievers (also called analgesia from Greek "without pain) are substances that we take to alleviate for a limited time period pain such as muscle or headaches. Common drugs are aspirin, ibuprofen, paracetamol. Many of those compounds have several names dependent on the brand and the manufacturer. Pain relievers are as old as humankind. For example ground White Willow bark, which later was found to contain a compound similar to aspirin, was used over the centuries for relief from pain and fever. Today we take both herbal remedies and synthetic drugs to suppress pain. The mechanisms of pain relievers in the body is still only poorly understood. Scientists know that some pain relievers act by blocking chemicals (prostaglandins), that simulate peripheral pain receptors to send a pain signal to the central nervous system.
