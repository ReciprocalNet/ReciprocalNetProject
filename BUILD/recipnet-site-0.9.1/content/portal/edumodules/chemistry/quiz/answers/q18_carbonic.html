Carbon monoxide
Sorry, this answer is incorrect. Carbon monoxide provides no protecting barrier for the earth's atmosphere. It is a dangerous pollutant that may contribute to the greenhouse effect and global climate change. Carbon monoxide is dangerous and life-threatening to humans and other forms of air-breathing life, as inhaling even small amounts can lead to neurological damage and possibly death. Carbon monoxide out-competes oxygen when binding to hemoglobin and therefore causes oxygen starvation throughout the body.
