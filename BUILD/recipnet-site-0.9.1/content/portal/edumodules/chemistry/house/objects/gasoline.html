Gasoline

Chemicals you may find:

Petroleum hydrocarbons, for example, paraffins, isoparaffins, cycloparaffins, n-hexane, benzene, methylcyclopentadienyl
p-Xylene , m-Xylene

Gasoline is a mixture of various chemicals that have been refined from crude oil. It is a colorless, light brown to pink liquid with a very characteristic odor. This substance is extremely flammable in liquid and vapor form but quickly evaporates when exposed to air. Gasoline is a powerful fuel used to power engines in cars, boats, lawn mowers, and other vehicles with internal-combustion engines. Gasoline has also been employed as a very dangerous explosive; it is one of the most dangerous substances found in the home. Improper removal of gasoline, through trash or sewer can cause fires and explosions or contaminates the waste and storm water.

Gasoline is also very harmful to one's health, inhalation, ingestion, absorption, and skin contact can cause poisoning that is characterized by slurred speech, staggering, and confusion. These symptoms may be followed by nausea, headache, and can possibly result in a coma and even death. To reduce the risk of gasoline exposure, the fuel should be kept out of reach of children and pets and should be stored in an approved safety container such as a metal gas can. Gasoline should be stored in a garage or an outbuilding with good ventilation and not in a car where it can explode on impact.
