Fire Extinguisher

Chemicals you may find:

Water
Carbon dioxide
Nitrogen



A fire extinguisher is a device that is used to put out fires in the case of an emergency. The extinguisher is composed of a pressurized canister generally containing dry chemicals, or others, that are easily dispersed when the valve is opened. Fire extinguishers are classified based on the type of fire that is to be put out. Class A fire extinguishers are used to put out fires that of ordinary items such as wood or paper and employ water and compressed gas to expel the flames. Class B extinguishers quench fires that involve flammable liquids such as gas or oil. Class C fire extinguishers are used to put out electrical fires. It is possible to buy combination fire extinguishers for use in situations where there are multiple flammable contents present.

The fire extinguishers more commonly used in kitchen and garage fires are classified as Type BC, and are composed of sodium or potassium bicarbonate. These dry chemicals are propelled by nitrogen or carbon dioxide to lower the temperature of the environment to a level of which the flammable reaction cannot continue.
