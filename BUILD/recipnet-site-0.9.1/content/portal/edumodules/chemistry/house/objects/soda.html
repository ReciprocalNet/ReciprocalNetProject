Soda Water

Chemicals you may find:

Water
Carbon dioxide
Carbonic acid
Citric acid
Salt (Sodium chloride)
Soda water (or sparkling water or simply seltzer) is carbonated water (water in which carbon dioxide was dissolved forming carbonic acid). It may also contain salt or citric acid or other flavors depending on the manufacturer. It is produced by passing pressurized carbon dioxide through water. When a soda bottle is opened pressure is released and carbon dioxide escapes and produces the distinctive bubbles. Carbonated water is the base for soft drinks where in addition sugars and flavors are added.
